Shared infrastructure Urban uses (all free & public)

  • Inflation adjustment: BLS CPI-U (real-dollar conversions to 2025 dollars). Public, monthly.
  • Geographic crosswalks: Missouri Census Data Center 2022 Geographic Correspondence Engine — maps PUMAs, zip codes, and counties to congressional districts. Public.
  • Population weights: US Census Bureau Population Estimates, used to roll county/state data up to national. Public, annual.
Bottom line: Urban's edge is granularity (congressional-district level) and a few proprietary inputs (ICE base prices, NielsenIQ grocery indices, and the Urban Credit Bureau Panel). The easiest wins for us are the fully public feeds — weekly earnings (BLS/FRED), Zillow rents, EIA electricity bills, and the FHFA house-price index — all of which give real dollar/level measures beyond the CPI indexes we currently rely on. Their proprietary layers (credit bureau panel, local grocery/home-price bases) are the hardest to reproduce, though we already carry public national equivalents for most of them.
Source: Airi, Braga, McKernan, Harvey & Acs, The American Affordability Tracker: Technical Appendix, Urban Institute, last updated April 2, 2026. View appendix (PDF).
